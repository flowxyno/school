Dragonlance: Dragons of Autumn Twilight

Book Synopsis:

Life-long friends, they went their separate ways. Now they are together again, though each holds secrets from the others in his heart. They speak of a world shadowed with rumors of war. They speak of tales with strange monsters, creatures of myth, creatures of legend. They do not speak of their secrets. Not then. Not until a chanced encounter with a beautiful, sorrowful woman who bears a magical crystal staff draws the companions deeper into the shadow forever changing their lives and shaping the fate of the world.

No one expected them to be heroes.

Least of all themselves.

Personal Review:

A friend of mine in high school recommended the Dragonlance series to me and this particular book as a starting point. This book series is definatly the perfect example of a fantasy series. A group of friends get back together after years apart and then get sucked into world spanning epic adventure, sounds like the majority of the D&D campaigns I have been apart of over the years. Though this series did have a few things in it that I had not seen in books up to this point (keep in mind I was reading Hardy Boys Mysteries up to this point in life as I was I think 13). One thing was the depth of the character builds, these characters had background, likes, dislikes, fears and their own secrets and plot twists. The second was when one of the main charcters dies (won't say which one in case you want to read it), which I had never seen.

Wheel of Time

Book Synopsis:

The Wheel of Time turns and Ages come and go, leaving memories that become legend. Legend fades to myth, and even myth is long forgotten when the Age that gave it birth returns again. In the Third Age, an Age of Prophecy, the World and Time themselves hang in the balance. What was, what will be, and what is, may yet fall under the Shadow.

When The Two Rivers is attacked by Trollocs—a savage tribe of half-men, half-beasts— five villagers flee that night into a world they barely imagined, with new dangers waiting in the shadows and in the light.

Personal Review:

The Wheel of Time series was and still is in my mind best epic fantasy series I have ever read, second only probably to the Hobbit and Lord of the Rings series. This series is most definatly not a light read. Spanning 14 books there is an amazing ammount of depth in this series that will have you really loving some characters and really hating (like Joffrey from Game of Thrones level hate) others. I can say that I identified with Rand (the primary character) off and on through out the entire series but other characters I could identify with through out. If you are looking for a series that will give you action, adventure, and excitement in a changing and evolving world background this is most definatly it. I was reading this series while the books were still coming out and it was usually one a year until the later books but I would have forgotten what happened in the series so I would re-read all the prior books and then read the new one. So fun fact is I think I picked the series up when it had 4 books out so following that pattern I have read this series 10 times though only once in its entirety.

If I had to say one thing that was bad about the series it would be that the characters were so detailed and and backgrounds so well mapped out that there would be almost entire books devoted to character development, but to me that was the only thing that would have been bad

Just so you are aware if you want to read the Wheel of Time series the Author Robert Jordan passed away in 2007 while in the middle of writing book 12 the series was finished (rather well in my opinion) by the Author Brandon Sanderson. So the series is finished but by a different author.

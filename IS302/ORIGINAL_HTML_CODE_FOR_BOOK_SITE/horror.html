Dracula

Book Synopsis:

The most famous of seductive evil in Western Literature, blood-thirsty Count Dracula has inspired countless movies, books, and plays. But, few, if any, have been fully to Bram Stoker's best-selling novel of mystery and horror, love and death, sin and redemption.

Written in the form of letters and diary entries, Dracula chronicles the vampire's journey from his Transylvanian castle to the nighttime streets of London. There, he searches for the blood he needs to stay alive - the blood of strong men and beautiful women - while his enemies plot to rid the world of his frightful power.

In Dracula, Stoker created a new word for terror, a new myth to feed our nightmares, and a character who will undoubtedly outlive us all.

Personal Review:

No horror book page would be complete with out Dracula! Out of all the horror books that I have read (and to be honest that isn't all that many) Dracula is the most recent and also the one that I had the most pre-conceptions about. When it comes to vampires most people think Dracula so being that I grew up that way I really thought I knew the book with out ever actually reading it. I was very pleasently surprised that I really only knew the very beginning of the book and the end so everything in the middle was new to me. The writing style was way different than anything I have read as it feels more like you are reading someone's journal or peeking at their letters which helped sell the old world feel of the book for me.

Personal Review:

It ... what to say about It, well the first time that I tried to read it I think I was a bit too young as I literally had to have a dictionary for every other paragraph. Then I tried reading it when I was like 12 I think, I still needed a dictionary but not as much. This book scared me enough that I crossed streets to get away from storm drains, didn't like clowns, and overall believed that things were out to eat me in the dark. So of course I had to see the movie with my best friend in high school which we did because neither one of us was going to say we were too scared to watch it. So this book and the following movie probably scarred my delicate young psyche so much that I had to watch the new version that came out this last couple of years. Awesome book really kind of let your mind do most of the work as far as what It was and most of the time your mind is way more demented than any movie screen. Don't really recommend it for children as young as I was but who knows ... see scarred :)

Pet Sematary

Book Synopsis:

When the Creeds move into a beautiful old house in rural Maine, it all seems too good to be true: physician father, beautiful wife, charming little daughter, adorable infant son - and now an idyllic home. As a family, they've got it all... right down to the friendly cat. But the nearby woods hide a blood-chilling truth - more terrifying than death itself... and hideously more powerful.

Personal Review:

Pet Sematary just reinforced me not being ok with any kind of graveyard because you just never know what is going to come out of it. Really well I enjoyed the book but remember saying to myself if you bury your cat in a graveyard and then it comes back to life but with a very bad disposition then why on earth would you do it again with something else, nothing good could possibly come out of that.

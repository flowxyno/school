Raise the Titanic

Book Synopsis:

The President's secret task force has developed an unprecedented defensive weapon that relies on an extremely rare radioactive element--and Dirk Pitt has followed a twisted trail to a secret cache of the substance. Now, racing against brutal storms, Soviet spies, and a ticking clock, Pitt begins his most thrilling mission--to raise from its watery grave the shipwreck of the century...

Personal Review:

This was literally the first book that I had ever read from the author Clive Cussler and oddly enough it is the 4th book in the series. I really enjoyed the main character Dirk Pitt for his nothing is impossible attitude. Clive Cussler books read like James Bond novels sometimes, but what fasinated me with them was the combination of history (as each book starts out with something in history), action and adventure, and a bit of mystery. Mr. Cussler has 4 different series that he writes all pretty much spun off of the Dirk Pitt series. If you like this kind of story line I highly recommend this series but I do recommend that you start at the beginning with the book The Mediteranean Caper as the books do build on one another in some aspects.

Jurassic Park

Book Synopsis:

An astonishing technique for recovering and cloning dinosaur DNA has been discovered. Now humankind’s most thrilling fantasies have come true. Creatures extinct for eons roam Jurassic Park with their awesome presence and profound mystery, and all the world can visit them—for a price.

Until something goes wrong. . . .

In Jurassic Park, Michael Crichton taps all his mesmerizing talent and scientific brilliance to create his most electrifying technothriller.

Personal Review:

For me this was an awesome book. I love practically anything that has to do with dionsaurs. It also featured a dinosaur that for some reason I had never really heard of (probably because I was to enamored with the T-Rex as a kid) the Velociraptor. Plot was a bit predictable as a dinosaur zoo, for the lack of a better term, would be way too much for murphys law to pass up so naturally it went down hill. Michael Crichton did an amazing job bringing to your mind the beauty, wonder and sheer imensity and terror there would be if you could interact with an extinct species such as dinosaurs. A fun piece of trivia is that the majority of Michael Crichton's books have gone to film.
